Playbooks: where AI pays for itself.

We go deep in a handful of industries where AI quietly pays for itself. For each: the work that’s bleeding time, what we’d build, and what changes once it’s live.

DETAIL /01PROPERTY MANAGEMENTSCALE 24/7

02:14“no heat — unit 4B”triaged emergency · dispatched

Every tenant handled — even the 11pm “no heat” call.

Tenants don’t message during office hours. Maintenance requests, lease questions and vendor chasing pile up overnight — and the real emergencies hide among the routine.

  • SPEC-1AI leasing assistant & 24/7 tenant support
  • SPEC-2Maintenance triage — routine vs. emergency
  • SPEC-3Vendor coordination & dispatch

AS-BUILT — auto-handles the bulk of inbound, escalates real emergencies in seconds (illustrative)

DETAIL /02REAL ESTATESCALE 24/7

18:22portal lead · 3BR, pre-approvedqualified · tour booked

Reply to every new lead in 30 seconds — not three hours.

Speed-to-lead wins deals, but agents are out showing homes while portal leads go cold. The follow-up that does happen is inconsistent.

  • SPEC-1Lead-qualification agent (budget, timeline, pre-approval)
  • SPEC-2SMS & email follow-up that books showings
  • SPEC-3Listing Q&A assistant

AS-BUILT — sub-minute first response; showings booked to the right agent (illustrative)

DETAIL /03LAW FIRMSSCALE 24/7

03:51after-hours injury callconflict-checked · consult booked

Capture the after-hours inquiry — book only the cases worth your time.

Most legal inquiries arrive after hours and go to whoever replies first. Meanwhile attorneys lose hours to intake and document chasing.

  • SPEC-124/7 intake & case qualification (jurisdiction, conflicts)
  • SPEC-2Document collection workflows
  • SPEC-3Fees & process FAQ

AS-BUILT — qualified, booked consults; the rest politely declined (illustrative)

DETAIL /04ACCOUNTING & CPASCALE 24/7

06:39“still need my W-2?”docs collected

Tax-season document chasing — handled, without one more reminder.

Onboarding and the endless “still waiting on your W-2” chase eat your busiest weeks. Clients mean well; they just need a nudge.

  • SPEC-1Client onboarding & document-chase agents
  • SPEC-2Scheduling & deadline reminders
  • SPEC-3Internal knowledge bot for staff

AS-BUILT — collection runs itself; files land ready to prep (illustrative)

DETAIL /05HOME SERVICES & TRADESSCALE 24/7

23:58missed call · furnace outcalled back · tech en route

Stop sending jobs to voicemail. Answer every call, book it, done.

For HVAC, plumbing and roofing, a missed call is a lost job — and they ring in while you’re on a roof. After-hours calls go straight to a competitor.

  • SPEC-124/7 voice agent that books the job
  • SPEC-2Dispatch, confirmations & reminders
  • SPEC-3Quote follow-up

AS-BUILT — every call answered; emergencies routed to the on-call tech (illustrative)

DETAIL /06FREIGHT & LOGISTICSSCALE 24/7

04:12“where’s my load #LT-4471?”ETA sent

Handle every “where’s my load?” — so dispatch can actually dispatch.

Brokers and carriers lose the day to status calls, carrier check-ins and appointment-setting — while the real exceptions, a detained truck or a missed pickup, hide in the noise.

  • SPEC-124/7 track-and-trace & shipment-status agent
  • SPEC-2Carrier check-in calls & appointment scheduling
  • SPEC-3Detention, POD chasing & exception flagging

AS-BUILT — routine status calls handled; dispatch hears only the real exceptions (illustrative)

What we build

Software that reads, writes, talks, listens and clicks the buttons.

Six ways we put AI to work — each one a real coworker doing a real job, wired into the tools you already use. Here's what each one is, in plain English, and where teams use it.

01 · Flagship

AI agents & coworkers

An agent doesn't just answer — it gets a job done. It plans the steps, uses your tools to do them, and knows when to stop and ask a human.

What it means: instead of a person doing a 12-step task, the agent does steps 1–11 and brings you step 12 to approve.

  • Onboard a new client end-to-end
  • Process a return and update stock
  • Triage and route an inbox
  • Prep a weekly report from your systems

02

Support that answers itself

A chat & email coworker trained on your help docs, policies and past tickets — resolving the routine and handing the rest to your team with the full thread.

Good for: order status, returns, FAQs, password resets, booking changes, "is this in stock?" — the questions you answer a hundred times a week.

03 · Voice · ASR + TTS

AI that answers the phone

A voice coworker that picks up, understands what people say, talks back naturally, and gets things booked. It listens (speech-to-text) and speaks (text-to-speech) in real time.

In plain terms: the line gets answered on the first ring, at 2pm or 2am, and the caller talks to something that actually helps — or gets put through to a person.

  • Book & confirm appointments
  • Qualify and route callers
  • Answer FAQs & hours
  • Take messages & call back

04

Back-office & documents

Reading paperwork and typing it into systems is exactly the kind of work AI is best at. Invoices, forms, receipts, applications — read, extracted, entered and reconciled.

The win: the data entry that eats your afternoons just gets done, with the odd exception flagged for a human glance.

05 · Knowledge

Custom copilots

An assistant that actually knows your business — trained on your documents, playbooks and history — living inside the tools your team already uses.

What it means: ask "what's our refund policy for wholesale?" or "draft a quote like the one we did for Acme" and get an answer grounded in your stuff, not the open internet.

06

Connect your stack

An agent is only useful if it can actually do things. We connect it — safely and with scoped permissions — to the tools you already run, so it can act, not just chat.

No rip-and-replace. Your team keeps the tools they like. The agent just learns to use them too.
